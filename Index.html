IELTS Reading (Memory)

⏱ 15:00
Memory is often perceived as a reliable record of past events. However, research in cognitive psychology has shown that human memory is highly reconstructive. Rather than retrieving exact copies of experiences, the brain actively rebuilds memories each time they are recalled. This process leaves memories vulnerable to distortion. External influences such as leading questions, social pressure, and even media exposure can alter recollections. As a result, individuals may confidently remember events that never actually occurred. One well-known experiment demonstrated that participants could be led to believe they had experienced childhood events that were entirely fabricated. Despite the lack of any real occurrence, many participants described these false memories in vivid detail. These findings have significant implications, particularly in legal contexts where eyewitness testimony is often considered crucial evidence.
